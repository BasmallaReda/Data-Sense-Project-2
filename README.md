# Consumer Preference Analysis: Local vs International Clothing Brands

This project presents a data analysis study exploring consumer preferences between local and international clothing brands. The analysis focuses on identifying key factors that influence purchasing decisions among young consumers.

# Overview

The study investigates how elements such as price, quality, brand perception, and shopping behavior affect consumer choices. It is based on survey data collected from university students and young adults aged 18–24.

# Objectives
Analyze whether consumers prefer local or international clothing brands

Identify the most influential factors in purchasing decisions

Understand shopping habits and behavior patterns among young consumers

# Dataset
Sample size: 50 participants

Data collected عبر online survey

Target group: university students and young adults

# Methodology

Convenience sampling

Statistical analysis including:

  Mean, median, and mode

  Distribution analysis

Visualization of key trends (shopping frequency, preferences, etc.)

# Key Insights

No clear dominance between local and international brands

Local brands are favored for:

  Lower prices

  Better value for money

International brands are preferred for:

  Perceived quality

  Brand trust

Consumers tend to balance between price and quality rather than choosing one category exclusively

# Limitations
Small sample size (50 responses)

Sampling bias (focused on students)

Subjective responses (perception-based answers)

# Conclusion

The results highlight a competitive balance between local and international brands. While international brands lead in perception, local brands remain strong due to affordability, making consumer decisions highly context-dependent.
